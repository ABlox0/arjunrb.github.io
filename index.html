Tuning Initative

What is our project?

                           

We are a community project aimed at teaching little kids at a daycare center how to play guitar. Our mission is to provide extracurricular activities that are often overlooked and undervalued. We noticed that the children at the daycare centre were very interested in the guitars; they were focused on the task at hand and allowed for free interaction. Despite the fact that the children had little musical experience, the sight of the guitars definitely excited them and drew their attention to us. Because our project is centred on music and instruments, the children were both auditory and visual learners.

What we did

                           

                               

  • Day 1: We went to Esperanza, surveyed the number of children and their interests towards music. We taught them what the guitar is, we taught them how to hold the guitar with correct posture, no. of strings. We finally ended the session with us playing songs to the kids and enjoying it with them.

    •                            

  • Day 2: Taught them what each string was named and showed them how to play the guitar while holding it. We ended the session by letting the kids take control of the guitar, let them play whatever they wanted.

    •                            

  • Day 3: We taught them what chords were and explained to them the structure of the guitar. We also had made them draw the guitar labelled completely based on their memory. We had asked them questions which they had answered correctly.

ABOUT US

ABOUT OUR PROJECT

At Tuning Initiative, we believe that every child deserves the opportunity to explore their creativity and learn new skills. We are a group of friends who are passionate about music and giving back to our community. We noticed that in many daycare centers, extracurricular activities, especially music, are not considered. So, we decided to start our own project called Tuning Initiative.

Our Services

                           

                           

                           

                           

The main service provided was teaching guitar to the little kids at the daycare center. The program was designed to be fun, interactive and engaging for the children. It was also focused on providing a positive and encouraging environment for the children to learn and develop their musical skills.

                           

We offer guitar lessons for kids at the daycare center. We are passionate about sharing their knowledge with the next generation of guitarists.

                               

  • Providing instruments: We provided instruments to the daycare center so that the children had access to them during the lessons. We made sure that the instruments were of good quality and appropriate for children to use.

    •                            

  • Music theory and history lessons: We included basic music theory and history lessons as part of the guitar lessons. This helped the children to better understand the music they were playing and develop a deeper appreciation for music in general.

    •                            

  • Performance opportunities: We organized small concerts and recitals for the children to perform in front of their parents and other members of the daycare center community. This helped to build their confidence and showcase their musical talents.

Statistics

                           

                           

                           

                           

Did you know that many schools and daycare centers have cut back on extracurricular activities in recent years? According to a recent study, 75% of schools have reduced time spent on arts education, while 95% of daycare centers do not offer any music education at all. At Tuning Initiative, we believe that music education is crucial for children's development, and we are committed to providing this opportunity to as many kids as possible. According to recent studies, only 20% of daycare centers offer music lessons to children. We believe that every child should have the opportunity to learn and enjoy music.

                               

  • According to a study conducted by the University of Kansas, students who participate in music programs, such as learning to play guitar, score higher on standardized tests and have higher grades in their classes compared to their peers who do not participate in music programs.

    •                            

  • A study by the National Endowment for the Arts found that students who participate in music programs are more likely to attend school, have higher rates of high school graduation, and are more likely to attend college than students who do not participate in music programs.

    •                            

  • Learning to play an instrument, such as guitar, can improve cognitive skills, such as memory and attention span. This can lead to improved academic performance and overall success in life.
